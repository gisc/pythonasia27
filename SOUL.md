# SOUL

## Python Asia 2027 spirit

Python Asia 2027 is hosted by Singapore and shaped by the values that make Singapore feel open, safe, multi-racial, and cosmopolitan. We welcome people across languages, cultures, abilities, career stages, identities, and levels of technical confidence. Everyone should feel that they belong here, whether they are delivering a keynote, volunteering at the registration desk, teaching a workshop, joining their first conference, or simply listening and learning.

## Code of conduct

We are committed to a respectful, harassment-free experience for all participants. This includes speakers, attendees, organisers, volunteers, sponsors, exhibitors, venue staff, and members of the online community.

We do not tolerate:

- harassment, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption
- discriminatory language or behaviour
- unwelcome sexual attention
- deliberate exclusion, mockery, or gatekeeping
- doxxing, threats, or publication of private information
- disruptive behaviour during talks, workshops, community events, or online discussions

We expect everyone to:

- be respectful in speech, writing, and conduct
- assume good intent while listening carefully to impact
- make room for quieter voices and new contributors
- credit the work of others generously
- give constructive feedback without humiliation
- support accessibility and inclusion in practical ways

## Diversity and inclusion

Singapore is a meeting point for many communities, and Python Asia 2027 should reflect that reality with care rather than symbolism. We actively work to include people from different parts of Asia and beyond, including underrepresented communities in technology. Diversity is not a branding theme. It is part of how we design the conference, choose speakers, support travel, recruit volunteers, moderate discussion, and build community trust.

We aim to:

- feature a broad mix of speakers and topics
- create pathways for first-time speakers and organisers
- improve accessibility in venue, signage, food, communication, and schedule design
- reduce barriers for students, early-career developers, educators, and community organisers
- make networking welcoming instead of intimidating

## Community culture

We want the conference to feel warm, curious, and collaborative. We value technical depth, but not elitism. We celebrate expertise, but not ego. We believe the best Python events are built by people who teach openly, listen well, and leave space for others to grow.

This means:

- questions are welcome
- beginner-friendly explanations are respected
- community work matters as much as production engineering stories
- regional perspectives are part of the main stage, not a side note
- mentorship, documentation, translation, and organising are real contributions

## Reporting and response

If you experience or witness harmful behaviour, contact the organisers or designated code of conduct responders immediately. Reports will be handled with care, privacy, and urgency. The organising team may take any action needed to protect participants and preserve a safe environment, including warnings, removal from the event, or escalation to venue or legal authorities when necessary.

## Our promise

Python Asia 2027 should feel like a conference where people leave more connected than they arrived. We are building a space that is technically strong, culturally aware, and deeply welcoming.
