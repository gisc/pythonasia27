# USER

## Community roles

Python Asia 2027 is built by many kinds of participants. Each role matters, and each role should feel recognised, supported, and clearly invited into the event.

## Speakers

Speakers bring knowledge, stories, experiments, cautionary lessons, and new questions. We welcome keynote speakers, first-time speakers, regional community leaders, maintainers, educators, researchers, developers, designers, policy thinkers, and builders working with Python in the real world.

We especially want talks from people who can share:

- practical Python engineering
- AI agent workflows and tooling
- data, machine learning, and responsible AI practice
- education and community outreach
- public interest technology
- open source maintenance
- stories from underrepresented regions and communities

## Partners

Partners help make the event sustainable, accessible, and ambitious. We invite ecosystem partners, education institutions, civic organisations, technology companies, startups, cloud providers, publishers, and community platforms that want to support the Python ecosystem in meaningful ways.

Good partnership at Python Asia 2027 means:

- supporting community growth without overwhelming it
- helping reduce participation barriers
- contributing to learning, not just brand visibility
- respecting the event code of conduct and community culture

## Volunteers

Volunteers are the human layer that makes the conference feel alive and cared for. They guide participants, solve problems, support accessibility, assist speakers, manage transitions, welcome newcomers, and help preserve the tone of the event.

Volunteers should receive:

- clear role descriptions
- training and briefing
- support from organisers
- recognition for their contribution
- a respectful and sustainable workload

## Organisers

Organisers are stewards of trust. Their role is to design an event that is logistically strong, financially responsible, ethically grounded, and emotionally welcoming. Organisers set the tone by making careful decisions about programme balance, accessibility, sponsorship, risk management, communication, and community care.

The organising approach for Python Asia 2027 should be:

- transparent where possible
- thoughtful under pressure
- regionally inclusive
- accountable to the community
- calm, kind, and prepared

## The shared user journey

Every participant should be able to answer these questions quickly:

- Why am I here?
- Where do I go next?
- Who can help me?
- How can I contribute?
- How do I stay connected after the event?

The website, programme, venue flow, volunteer system, and communication design should all support that journey.
